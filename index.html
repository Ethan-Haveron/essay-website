Jessica Hische

lettering artist, illustrator and author.

Jessica hische is a lettering artist, illustrator and author that has done work with the likes of penguin books. On top of this she is also a public speaker that talks at universities and conferences. For example she has done talks at the “American Craft Council” and Lipscomb University about her life and career within lettering. In a treehouse interview, will Jessica she describes lettering as “lettering is a very weird mix of graphic design and illustration”. She also describes lettering as basically a onetime thing that’s specific to the project and the lettering will not be reproduces for other projects.

Jessica grew up in Hazelton Pennsylvania, Jessica always wanted to be an artist, she stated in an interview that she “was a kid that just loved to draw all day.” Jessica’s parents always let her draw because it was a “quiet activity” that kept her busy for long periods of the day. In college Jessica took many subjects within art eg fine art, glassblowing, painting. Eventually she found her path after her first design class.

Jessica didn’t have enough money to buy appealing fonts for her projects in college and she didn’t want to spend ages scrolling through multiple websites looking at boring fonts. Jessica thought it would be better if she just created the letters herself. The more she drew the more she noticed her work standing out from the rest of the students in her class. By the end of college Jessica had a sizable amount of lettering work in her portfolio.

Jessica managed to land a job working for Louise Fili at the age of 23 from sending out some of her work to many different companies and high up designers in firms including Louise. Obviously, she got hardly any responses apart from Louise who was open to the idea of bringing in new people.

From start to finish Jessica was working on lettering for book covers, logos, magazines etc . Jessica still having no knowledge that that’s what she was doing. Jessica just thought that was a part of her illustration and design work. This just shows that there are many levels within design that people do not know about and just accept as graphic design or another term that is widely spread around or easier to understand.

While Jessica was working for Louise, Jessica was also doing some freelance work on the side. Louise and Jessica worked out an agreement for her to work for her and let Jessica do her own thing if it didn’t interfere with the work that Jessica is doing for Louise.

She then worked for her for two and a half years before going full time as a freelance designer. In an interview, Jessica said that she got more money from her freelance work that what she did while working for Louise, also it allowed her to get some of her personal time back so she could do her own thing.

Right after Jessica quit working for Louise, she started a project called the daily drop cap, this project that she made for herself was only to make sure that she was drawing letter forms every day. little did she know that this would open many more paths for her career and benefit her greatly overall as a designer and a lettering artist.

In this project, Jessica drew a letter a day until she completed 12 alphabets. Jessica posted all her letters to her website for everyone to see. This brought a lot of attention over to her site, to see all these different letters that she has done and to see what she has done in the past etc. Below each post Jessica added a bit of code that allowed people to use these drop caps in their own sites, blogs etc.

This is the time that she started to get asked to go to different places for talks and different public speaking events.

From this Jessica was given a large project from ‘Penguin’ called Penguin Drop Caps. Within this project Jessica was hired to letter design 26 book covers each book with a cap that was based on the Author’s last name.

The designs on these books where not just copied over from her original “daily drop cap” project, but each cover was created with this large cap in the centre of the cover with elements around it or the elements made the letter depending on the content of the book.

“The work you do while you procrastinate is probably the work you should be doing for the rest of your life.”

What a beautiful quote, I can also relate to this as I myself do small designs when I procrastinate but I never realised it until I seen this. I know I should be doing more important things but I like knowing that I’ve made something that I like and that others will also find appealing.

Lettering and illustration work Jessica will always start of with pencil sketches

“My pencils used to be quite rough but because I’ve been doing more and more lettering work for advertising clients, they’ve become more refined.”

Starting with sketching is not her preferred way to work but clients need to approve of the work so far before she can move onto illustration/lettering based programmes. After the initial sketches, have been approved she then moved over to illustrator and recreates the sketch. Jessica doesn’t simply scan the sketch in and trace the image, as she feels that creating the final piece of work without tracing helps her correct any mistakes that she may have made.

When Jessica uses illustrator she mainly uses the pen tool as she feels that once you master the pen tool you basically master illustrator. While designing Jessica only uses mouse and not a Wacom tablet as she is use to practising with a mouse for many years and feels that she works faster with using a mouse.

Jessica published a book called “in Progress” which is a book that shows the process of the design world and the creative thinking behind the brain of Jessica hische. The book shows Jessica’s sketches and process in a resource driven fashion, for large clients such as Wes Anderson, NPR and Starbucks.

It all started off by ‘chronical’ contacted Jessica and suggested that she show write a book, Jessica wanted to do one but didn’t know how to base her skills into a book as she didn’t want to create a monograph book (combination of life’s work).

Her first thought on the book was to create a book that showed the process of some of her work, from the initial sketch to the finish product. As she further worked with the publishers the book went from a process to a resource style book. Over time she realised that this book was a very important thing (more that she first thought) so Jessica wanted to make it as resourceful and dense as possible for readers. The booked changed from 5-10 thousand words, which was just her own notes beside her designs etc to 30-40 thousand words that goes more in depth about the information shown on each page.

Today Jessica divides her very intensive lifestyle between Brooklyn and San Francisco with her husband and child. Jessica also runs her studio and has a workshop area for people to learn different skills within lettering and design.

When she isn’t working, she is “Procrastiworking” Jessica always uses her free time with side projects, usually web based projects, such as ‘how to use Twitter’ and a website to share knowledge that she has picked up. When she isn’t doing any type of working Jessica likes to spend time with friends and family like every other person, Jessica also spends time travelling to different conferences and doing public speaking talking about her life so far.
